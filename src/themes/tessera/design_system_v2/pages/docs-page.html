Patterns

A pattern is the shape of a section. It tells Tessera which rows to fetch, how to fetch them, and how to render them. The same primitive that backs a homepage block also backs a chart, a table, or a map.

Patterns are stored as ordinary rows in the patterns table. They reference a dataset, a query, and a rendering. A pattern is small — the rendering is just metadata. The data is somewhere else.

note

Patterns are typed rows. Anything you can do with a row — version, audit, snapshot, query — you can do with a pattern.

Anatomy of a pattern §

A pattern is a typed row with four required fields and several optional ones. The minimum schema below is abbreviated; consult the migration in 0014_patterns.sql for the canonical definition.

Slugs are namespaced: station.now_playing, civic.indicator_grid, archive.bibliography. The first segment denotes the site, the second denotes the pattern's role within that site.

Kinds §

Tessera ships five built-in renderings. Custom renderings may be registered by a theme; see Themes → Custom renderings.

Table §

A typed grid. Tabular figures, alternating row tints, hairline dividers. The most common pattern; the workhorse of the system.

Calendar §

A grid keyed on a time column. Hour-of-day on the horizontal axis, day-of-week on the vertical. Custom keying via calendar.key_x and calendar.key_y.

Prose §

A single typed row treated as long-form. Use this for "About" pages, theory essays, and any surface where the row is the page.

Composing a page §

A page is itself a row of kind page that points at a list of patterns. The page does not store its sections; it stores their slugs. This is the central move: composition by reference, not by embedding.

Publishing §

Publishing snapshots a page and its constituent patterns into an immutable revision. Old revisions are addressable; rollback is a row insert.
