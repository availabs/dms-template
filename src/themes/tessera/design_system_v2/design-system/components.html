lexical — rich text rendering

The shape of your data is the shape of your site

A tessera is a single tile in a Roman or Byzantine mosaic — the smallest unit of a deliberately composed surface. Roman craftsmen spent careers placing tesserae one at a time into floors and ceilings that lasted millennia.

One representation, many renderings

Every page, every section, every row, every chart is a tessera — small, typed, ordinary on its own. The site is the mosaic that emerges when many of them compose against a shared form.

A page is a row. A chart is a row. A map is a row. There is nothing else to learn.

The closest intellectual parallel is Excel — not in the spreadsheet sense, but in the representation-primacy sense. Excel's power isn't its formula language; it's that it has only sheets, columns, rows, and cells. Reference it as data_items.

  • A theme is a row whose data is style.
  • A page is a row whose children are sections.
  • A section is a row.
