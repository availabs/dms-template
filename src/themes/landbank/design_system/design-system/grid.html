Structure · Columns

Twelve columns, twelve-pixel gutters.

The column grid every section sits on inside any band — the survey grid under every page. The grid itself is gap-0; the gutter is each section's own padding; the card chrome lives on an inner box. One grid for the whole system, no freehand widths.

grid-cols-12 · gap-0 gutter = p-3 (12px) cap max-w-[1240px] · centered

The grid spec

Every LayoutGroup on every page hosts the same sectionArray grid. These are the settings — memorize the first four and you can read any page in this system.

The gutter model

gap-0 · padding gutters · inner-box chrome. The grid never owns the space between sections — each section carries its own p-3, and the card you see is an inner box drawn inside that padding.

Two adjacent sections, padding zones exposed
Section A

The inner box — border, 6px radius, white ground — is the card you see. The section wrapper around it stays invisible.

Section B

Its neighbor's padding meets ours at the column boundary — no grid gap anywhere in between.

12 + 12 = 24px
section padding = gutter (p-3)
inner box carries border + radius + bg

Why gap-0: a grid gap is the same for every pair of neighbors and can't be removed for just one edge. Padding belongs to the section, so any single edge can go to zero — which is exactly what lets two sections fuse into one visual card. Content padding inside a card (p-6) is the component's own and is never the gutter.

Compound cards — the worked example

The property ledger on the dashboard looks like one card. It is two sections: a header and a table, fused by zeroing the padding on their shared edge and coordinating corners. Rendered live below, in miniature.

Section A · px-3 pt-3 pb-0

The header piece keeps top and side gutters but zeroes the bottom. Its inner box drops the bottom edge: rounded-b-none border-b-0.

Shared edge · pb-0 meets pt-0

Both paddings on the seam are zero, so the two inner boxes butt together. The header's bottom border is gone; the table's own top border is the one hairline between them.

Section B · px-3 pt-0 pb-3

The body piece zeroes its top and squares its top corners: rounded-t-none. Outer corners stay 6px, so the fused stack reads as one card.

The rule: a compound card is adjacent sections with their shared-edge padding zeroed and corners coordinated — top piece rounded-b-none border-b-0, bottom piece rounded-t-none. It maps 1:1 to two DMS sections; nothing here is a bespoke component.

Responsive collapse

Spans step down at breakpoints; nothing ever leaves the 12-column system. A collapsed tile is just a bigger span of the same grid.

Row base · < 768 md · ≥ 768 lg / xl · ≥ 1024 / 1280
KPI tiles col-span-6 · two across md:col-span-4 · three across xl:col-span-2 · six across
Map + donut col-span-12 · stacked still stacked lg:col-span-8 + lg:col-span-4
Charts row col-span-12 · stacked md:col-span-6 · the 4 and 3 pair up lg:col-span-5 + 4 + 3

The section gutter never changes — p-3 at every breakpoint — so collapsed layouts keep the same rhythm as the full grid.

Rules for picking spans

  • Default to 12. A section earns a narrower span only when it genuinely shares a row — never just to look smaller.

  • KPI tiles are 2 — six across the cap, collapsing to three then two. Don't stretch six-across spacing over sparse data; four stats read better at 3.

  • Primary canvas + sidecar is 8 + 4 (the map and its donut). Two equal peers are 6 + 6.

  • 5 + 4 + 3 only when three cards have genuinely different densities — a wide time series, a medium breakdown, a narrow list. Otherwise 4 + 4 + 4.

  • No row-spans. Vertical rhythm comes from stacking rows; cards equalize height with h-full inside their row.

  • Never freehand widths. If a span isn't in the vocabulary on this page, it doesn't ship — add it here first or take the nearest span that is.
