About · Our mission

A public authority with one job: return vacant property to the neighborhood.

The Albany County Land Bank is a not-for-profit corporation dedicated to revitalizing neighborhoods by addressing vacant properties. We acquire tax-foreclosed, vacant, or abandoned properties throughout Albany County and identify responsible buyers through an application process that includes multi-layered review.

The basics

What a land bank is — and isn't

Land banks are creatures of New York's 2011 Land Bank Act — public authorities that counties and cities can charter to take on the properties the private market has walked away from. Albany County established its land bank in 2014 as the county's answer to a growing backlog of tax-foreclosure vacancy: blocks where title problems, back taxes, and deterioration kept parcels from ever reaching a responsible owner.

Despite the name, a land bank is not a bank. It doesn't lend, and it doesn't hold land hoping it appreciates. It does three things, over and over: it acquires vacant, abandoned, and tax-foreclosed parcels; it stabilizes them — boarding, mowing, clearing title; and it resells them to buyers with a credible plan to put them back to use.

That narrow job is the whole design. When a parcel no longer needs the Land Bank, it leaves the portfolio — through the pipeline below — and the neighborhood gets a taxpaying property back.

NYS Land Bank Act: Article 16 of the Not-for-Profit Corporation Law, enacted 2011.

At a glance

Established 2014 by the Albany County Legislature.

1,314 properties acquired since 2015 — over 92% through county tax foreclosure.

Governed by a volunteer Board of Directors — meetings open to the public.

Reports to Albany County and New York State — audits and annual reports published.

The pipeline

From foreclosure to front porch

Three steps, repeated parcel by parcel — more than a thousand times over the past decade.

01
Acquire

Nearly everything arrives through Albany County tax foreclosure — over 92% of acquisitions since 2015. Taking title stops vacant parcels from cycling through speculation and neglect.

02
Stabilize

Crews and vendors board structures, mow lots, and keep up routine maintenance while staff clear titles — so every parcel is safe, legal, and ready to transfer.

03
Return

Application-based sales match parcels with responsible buyers; in-house rehab projects and co-development clusters take on the blocks that need more than a sale.

Accountability

Built to be watched

The Land Bank is a public authority. Its board meets in public, its minutes and resolutions are published, and its records are available under the Freedom of Information Law.

Board & committees

A seven-member volunteer board appointed by the County Legislature, with Governance, Finance, and Real Estate committees.
Annual reports

Filed with the New York State Authorities Budget Office and published every year, alongside independent audits.
Board meetings

Monthly and open to the public, with notice posted ahead of time and minutes published after.
Policies & resolutions

The disposition pricing policy and every sale resolution, published — how each price is set is on the record.
Partners

This work is shared.

None of it happens alone. Albany County moves foreclosed parcels into the pipeline; the City of Albany coordinates code enforcement and planning; the New York Land Bank Association connects the work to a statewide movement; and community partners tell us which blocks need attention first.
