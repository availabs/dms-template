From application to closing

Buying from the Land Bank, in four steps

Properties aren't sold to the fastest bidder. Every application gets a multi-layered review so properties go to buyers with a real plan — that's the point of a land bank.

01
Browse the inventory

Search the portfolio by neighborhood, price, and property type. Every listing shows the parcel survey, zoning, and lot dimensions.

02
Apply with your plan

Submit a purchase application with your intended use and proof of funds. Staff hold weekly application-support sessions if you want help.

03
Multi-layered review

Staff and the board review feasibility, neighborhood fit, and buyer readiness. Approved sales go to resolution at a public board meeting.

04
Close and build

Sign the purchase agreement, close, and put the property back to work — a garden, a side yard, a rehab, a new home.

Where the work is

Block by block, a decade of returns

Since 2015 the Land Bank has moved more than a thousand properties out of vacancy — concentrated where tax foreclosure hit hardest: West Hill, the South End, and Arbor Hill. The full portfolio, every parcel, is public.
