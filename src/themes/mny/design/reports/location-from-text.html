The result

The descriptions knew where
the work was. Now the map does too.

The Actions Data Quality audit found the map 96.7% complete but 99.6% of its points centroids — the middle of a town, or of a whole county. Only 74 of 18,378 actions sat where the work would actually happen. The structured location fields were empty (1.2% had an address); the conclusion was that precision is a data-collection problem no code can fix.

Half true. The free text wasn’t empty — the sites were right there in the names and descriptions: “Public Water Well flood mitigation (7737 Main St),” “Route 10 — Third Brook Bridge Replacement,” “8960 Route 31 Culvert Upsizing (43.07152, −76.95832).” So we mined every centroid action’s text and geocoded it.

Using the NY State geocoding service, we recovered a real coordinate for 2,399 of the 17,695 centroid actions and staged them into the field the pass reads. The NYS street-name locator geocodes a road with no house number — something the Census geocoder can’t do — which is what lifted the yield from ~380 to 2,399. Site-accurate points go from 74 to ~2,473, a 33× jump. 116 of them were explicit lat/longs sitting in descriptions the pass never reads today.

And they land where the work is: a median 3.9 km off the old centroid (up to 69 km) — an action pulled off the middle of its county onto its actual street. Most of the gain (2,066 of 2,399) is in jurisdiction-centroid actions; the 332 county-centroid recoveries move the farthest.

Not all 2,399 are equally sure. 702 are high/medium confidence — a coordinate, a street address, a numbered route, or an intersection. The other 1,697 are named roads: geocoded to the right road name in the right county, but a generic name like “South Street” can match the wrong same-named road. A distance gate keeps every one in-county (90% within 13 km), and they’re a conscious trade of some precision for a lot of coverage.

702 high/medium + 1,697 named road = 2,399 applied, +129 held for review. Geocoder split: NYS street-name 1,952 · NYS address 207 · Census 127 · parsed 113. The named-facility tier (bridges, plants, dams) was left for a future pass — it needs a place-name gazetteer the NYS locators don’t cover.

Read the named-road tier with care

1,697 rows (71% of the total) are single generic street names. They geocode to the right road name and the gate keeps them in-county, but the tail may be the wrong same-named road. Applying only the 702 high/medium is the conservative option.

What we built · what’s left

Recovered & staged — one step from live

✓ Mined coordinates & addresses

116 explicit coordinates pulled from descriptions the pass ignores, plus 216 addresses and 118 intersections geocoded. Written into geometry_lat_long_polygon_etc, and address_if_available filled where blank (199 rows).

✓ Roads via the NYS geocoder

The NY State street-name locator unlocked 255 numbered routes and 1,697 named roads — the tier the Census geocoder couldn’t touch. This is the whole reason the yield is 2,399, not ~380.

→ Apply & re-run the pass

The write script is built and dry-run-validated (backs up first, idempotent). One --apply to the actions source, then re-run actions_location/publish and the 2,399 become precision 1. Not yet applied — pending sign-off.

→ The long tail

Named facilities (bridges, plants, dams) still need a place-name gazetteer. And a pipeline tweak — have rung 1 read the name/description too — would place future actions with in-text coordinates automatically.

The ceiling is still the intake form. Text mining recovers what planners already wrote; it can’t invent a location that was never described. The actions with no usable signal — and every future action — only become site-accurate if address or coordinates are captured at entry. But mining the existing text turned 74 real locations into ~2,473, from data already in hand.
