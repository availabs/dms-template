Overview

New York State faces a diverse array of hazards, from natural events like floods and hurricanes to the accelerating impacts of climate change, followed by the aftermath of these disasters. Effective hazard mitigation requires understanding and assessing these risks and their potential impacts to develop strategies to reduce vulnerability.

Climate Change

Anthropogenic climate change is already impacting New York and will continue to do so in the future. Hazard mitigation plans must account for climate change in order to effectively reduce the loss of life and property.

People and Communities

According to the Census Bureau, in 2022, more than 3.3M adults in the U.S. were forced to evacuate their homes because of hurricanes, floods, and other disasters.

Plan to Act

As climate change continues to put New York's people, property, and ecosystems at risk, the 2023 State Hazard Mitigation Plan, 58 Local Hazard Mitigation Plans, and the MitigateNY 2.0 project are working together.
