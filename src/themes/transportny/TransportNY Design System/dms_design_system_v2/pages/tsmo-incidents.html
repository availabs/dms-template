It isn't the count — it's the delay.

This page covers the reactive incidents operations responds to — crashes, road hazards, weather, emergencies — not planned construction, which lives on the work-zones dashboard. Most of the lost time comes from a small number of long, lane-blocking events; we measure each one's delay footprint directly and price it at a vehicle-class-weighted value of time.

// methodology
How incidents are measured

Event counts and categories come from TRANSCOM's multi-state feed, filtered to New York and classified by nysdot_sub_category. Construction is excluded — it's a planned-work story on the work-zones dashboard — so this page is the reactive incidents operations responds to. Delay is attributed per event by joining its affected TMCs to NPMRDS probe speeds and subtracting the recurrent baseline; about one in five events carries a measurable footprint. Dollars use a vehicle-class-weighted value of time — passenger $52, single-unit truck $42, combination truck $77 per vehicle-hour — weighted by each segment's AADT truck mix (≈$53 blended, higher on freight corridors). Clearance thresholds are computed only on the 42.7% of events with real lanes-open timestamps, with coverage shown alongside; secondary-crash tracking is on the roadmap.
