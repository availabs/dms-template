// 04 How LOTTR works

One ratio, four periods, person-mile weighted.

The ratio
80th ÷ 50th percentile travel time

For every TMC segment, LOTTR compares a bad day to a normal day. A ratio of 1.50 means a trip that normally takes 20 minutes takes 30 on a bad day. Computed separately for four federal periods: AM peak (6–10a), Midday (10a–4p), PM peak (4–8p) weekdays, and Weekend (6a–8p).

The test
Reliable = under 1.50 in all four

A segment passes only if every period stays under 1.50 — one bad period makes it unreliable. That's why the map colors each segment by its worst period, and why the period toggle matters: the same road can be green all morning and red at 5pm.

The weighting
People, not pavement

The statewide number is the share of person-miles on reliable segments: length × directional AADT × average vehicle occupancy. High-volume urban segments dominate the score — which is how 8% of the mileage can hold 18% of the person-miles hostage, and why the NYC metro drives the statewide result.
