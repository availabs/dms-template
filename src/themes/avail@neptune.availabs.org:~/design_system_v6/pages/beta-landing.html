what's inside

It already does more than you'd guess

I've been quietly building this for a while. It's not a toy — three real sites run on it today. Here's the honest tour.

a grid you can feel

Every page is sections on a 12-column sheet. Drag them, span them, stack them. Toggle the grid on and it looks like graph paper because it basically is.

your data, live

Upload a CSV or a shapefile and it becomes a real dataset with versions. Cards, tables, and charts bind to it — update the data, every page updates.

tables that behave

Sort, filter, paginate, search across sixteen columns at once. A keyword box over 17k rows is a config option, not a feature request.

charts without a chart guy

Pick a dataset, pick an x and a y, get a chart in the site's palette. Target lines are just a second series — no bespoke anything.

maps, actual maps

GIS data renders as real vector maps — parcels, corridors, county lines. A land bank runs its whole property viewer on this.

themes all the way down

Every button, nav, and card reads from a theme. Swap it and the same content becomes a different site. This page is a theme. So is the dark mode you can toggle up top.

how a page happens

Place, bind, publish

01 · place

Drag a section onto the sheet

Text, card, table, graph, map — pick one, drop it, give it columns. The grid does the layout math so you don't.

02 · bind

Point it at a dataset

Choose the source, choose the columns, choose how each one renders — image, link, formatted number, whatever. The section fills itself.

03 · publish

Hit the button

Drafts stay drafts until you say so. Publish puts the page live; your visitors never see your half-finished experiments. Undo exists.

faq

Questions you'll ask me anyway

is it really free?

While it's a beta, yes. If it ever costs money, everyone on the beta keeps a free plan. You're doing me the favor here.

what if you get bored of it?

Your content is yours — pages and datasets export as JSON and CSV whenever you want. No hostages.

what's it built on?

React and Postgres, with a big data layer for the map/chart stuff. There's a CLI too, tessera page create, if you're that kind of friend.

will an update eat my site?

Published pages are immutable snapshots — an update can break the editor (see rough edges), but not your live site. That part I'm careful about.

Come build something weird.

A bird log. A zine archive. Your rec-league standings with an actual live table. The best beta testers are the ones with strange ideas.
