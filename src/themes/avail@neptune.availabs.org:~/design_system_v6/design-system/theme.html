tessera design system · v6 · graph paper

Theme — tokens

The brand's vocabulary before it composes into anything: thirteen named colors in two lamps (light and dark), fifteen type tokens in four voices, a drawn icon set, and the 24px sheet that appears where sections land. If a token is missing here, it's missing everywhere.

every color is a CSS variable — flip the moon up top and watch nothing break ↗

01 · color

Two lamps, one drawer of names

Desk-drawer names, never numbered steps. Each token is a custom property (--t-paper, --t-cobalt, …) with a light and a dark value; utility classes stay identical in both modes. Cobalt is the only accent that speaks — marker highlights, go/amber/brick are status, everything else is neutral.

02 · type

Fifteen tokens, four voices

IBM Plex Sans carries display and prose — Zed's own family (Zed Plex is customized IBM Plex); display is weight and tracking, not a second face. IBM Plex Mono is the chrome voice: eyebrows, badges, code, tables of fact. Caveat is the margin voice — handwritten notes from the builder, rationed, never UI. Modifier axes (color, italic, tabular-nums, uppercase) are applied at the call site and never mint tokens.

06 · the sheet

Graph paper, grain, and drafting furniture

The page is plain paper with a fine grain (feTurbulence, in _shared.css). The 24px graph grid is rationed: hero and CTA bands get the fading sheet, editor canvases and demo surfaces get the full sheet, everything else stays paper. Everywhere, the grid is wallpaper; where sections land, it's the drafting sheet.

t6-sheet-fade

hero/CTA overlay — the grid dissolves into paper

t6-sheet

the grid surface, embeddable in panels

t6-hatch

45° hatching, the raw material of strips and dividers

edge patterns

hatch · backhatch · dashes · ticks · rungs — one flavor per marketing band, 16px at the viewport edge

t6-divider

hatched band separator, square joint tiles at its ends

t6-joint

a 7px square tile pinned where rules meet — the joints of the sheet

the important bit, marked

t6-mark

highlighter swipe — ≤ 2 per band

Usage rules: the grid appears on at most two bands per page — heroes/CTAs via the fading sheet, plus any true canvas (editor, demo surfaces); never as a page-wide background. Hatched t6-dividers separate marketing bands (Layout default); product pages stay quiet. Every marketing band carries ONE edge pattern connecting its dividers — hero/CTA keep the hatch, content bands rotate backhatch/dashes/ticks/rungs, the board band uses chalk ticks. The sticky TopNav carries the hatch rails too — the header rides the rails as the page scrolls. Joint squares sit only where rules meet. Always 16px at the viewport edge, never wider, never inside cards. Marker never touches UI controls — it's annotation, not state. The handwritten note voice follows the same rule: margins, not chrome.

07 · dark mode

The desk lamp off

One markup, two lamps

Every color utility resolves to a custom property, so dark mode is data-theme="dark" on <html> — no dark: variants, no second markup. The toggle stores the choice in localStorage["t6-theme"]; an early head script applies it before first paint; unset falls back to prefers-color-scheme.

<html data-theme="dark"> // that's the whole API

Rules that change in the dark

  • ·cobalt lightens to #7B9CFF so links keep AA
  • ·marker drops to a 28% film — highlighter on black paper
  • ·grid lines flip to chalk at 4.5%; grain stays
  • ·board bands keep their own tokens — they don't invert
  • ·shadows deepen; borders still do the depth work

08 · voice

A friend showing you a thing they made

say

  • "I'm building a website builder. Want to break it?"
  • "Publish puts your draft live. That's it."
  • "Maps are slow on big datasets right now — working on it."

don't say

  • "Unlock the power of composable content infrastructure."
  • "Submit" (say what happens: "Save draft", "Publish")
  • "Oops! Something went wrong 😅" (say what broke and what to do)

Sentence case everywhere, including headlines. Plain verbs. Numbers are real numbers. Errors name the problem and the fix. The beta admits what's broken — the "known rough edges" band is a brand pattern, not an apology.
